Dial test for knee injury
The dial test is performed to help detect an injury to the posterolateral corner of the knee. External rotation of the tibiaÂ is compared while the knees are flexed to 30 degrees and to 90 degrees. A difference of more than 10 to 15 degrees of external rotation compared with the uninjured side constitutes a positive test.
