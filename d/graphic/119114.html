Magnetic resonance imaging of postnatal lissencephaly
Panel A: Classical (type I) lissencephaly is characterized by the loss of the folds of the sulci and gyri, an abnormally thick cortex, and loss in cortical lamination.
Panel B: Cobblestone (type II) lissencephaly consists of cobblestone cortex, abnormal white matter, and enlarged ventricles. A thin lining composed of ectopic neurons (arrows), which have migrated beyond the superficial marginal zone, is seen on the surface of the brain. Cobblestone lissencephaly is usually associated with eye malformations and congenital muscular dystrophy.
Panel C: Normal brain for comparison.
