Platelet sequestration and consumption in placenta
Placental morphology and immunohistochemical staining show areas of platelet consumption in normal placenta. These sections were obtained at the time of delivery.
(A) Low-magnification view,hematoxylin and eosin staining, mature terminal villi. Thick arrowspoint toregions of perivillous fibrinoid.
(B) Section adjacentto that in (A)stained with an antibody to platelet membrane glycoprotein IIIa (anti-CD61, brown color). Thin arrows point to platelets in a linear pattern within the perivillous fibrinoid.
(C) Higher magnificationshowing amature terminal villus. Dashed arrow indicates perivillous fibrinoid sealing a gap in the syncytiotrophoblast surface.
(D) Section adjacent to that in (C) stained with an antibody toshowplatelets. Arrowhead points to plateletswithin the perivillous fibrinoid.
From: Reese JA, Peck JD, Yu Z, et al. Platelet sequestration and consumption in the placental intervillous space contribute to lower platelet counts during pregnancy.
