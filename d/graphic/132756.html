Example of a network diagram from a network meta-analysis: Interventions for acute diarrhea in children
The network diagram visually conveys the size and complexity of the network in NMA. This example shows the network diagram for an NMA that evaluated 62 clinical trials (20,256 participants) evaluating different treatments for acute diarrhea in children. The "nodes" (blue dots) represent different treatments; the size of each node corresponds to the number of participants who received that treatment. The "edges" (black lines connecting different pairs of dots) represent trial(s) directly comparing the 2 treatments; the thickness of the line corresponds to the number of trials.
NMA: network meta-analysis; ALL-PRB: all probiotics, except LGG and SB; CAO: kaolin-pectin; DM: diluted milk; LCF: lactose-free formula; LCF+PRB: lactose-free formula plus probiotics; LGG: Lacticaseibacillus rhamnosus GG; LGG+SM: LGG plus smectite; LOP: loperamide; MN: micronutrients; PRE: prebiotics; RC: racecadotril; SB: Saccharomyces boulardii; SB+LCF: S. boulardii plus lactose-free formula; SB+ZN: S. boulardii plus zinc; SB+ZN+LCF: S. boulardii plus zinc plus lactose-free formula; SM: smectite; SM+ZN: smectite plus zinc; STND: standard treatment or placebo; SYM: symbiotics; SYM+LCF: symbiotics plus lactose-free formula; VA: vitamin A; YOG: yogurt; YOG+PRB+ZN: yogurt plus probiotics plus zinc; ZN: zinc; ZN+LCF: zinc plus lactose-free formula; ZN+MN: zinc plus micronutrients; ZN+PRB: zinc plus probiotics.
