Diagnostic evaluation for functional impairment
Order of assessment Focus of evaluation Diagnostic focus
Characterize the disability Whole person
  • Time course (eg, insidious versus rapid)
  • Associated symptoms (eg, associated pain and its location, shortness of breath, fatigue)
  • Specific activities affected (eg, walking, bathing, toileting)
  • Compensatory strategies in use (eg, assistive devices, human help, reducing frequency, spiritual/cognitive strategies)
  • Define severity/extent of disability (eg, physical performance test, self-report ADL/IADL function)
Identify contributing impairments Organ system
  • Sensory impairment (eg, vision, tactile, vestibular)
  • Nutrition (eg, weight change, temporal wasting)
  • Cardiopulmonary (eg, shortness of breath, chest pain)
  • Musculoskeletal (eg, strength, flexibility, balance)
  • Cognitive disorders (eg, dementia, mild cognitive impairment)
Identify underlying conditions Pathophysiology
  • Mood disorders (eg, depression, delirium)
  • Neurologic conditions (eg, neuropathy, stroke, parkinsonism)
  • Musculoskeletal conditions (eg, osteoarthritis, osteoporosis)
  • Endocrine conditions (eg, thyroid, diabetes)
  • Eye diseases (eg, cataracts, macular degeneration)
  • Medication adverse effects (eg, orthostatic hypotension)
  • Miscellaneous conditions (eg, anemia, frailty/sarcopenia)
  • Social support (eg, availability/involvement of caregiver)
Identify contextual factors Personal and environmental context
  • Financial (eg, insurance, veteran benefits, personal finances)
  • Physical environment (eg, weather, terrain, steps/doorways)
  • Personal (eg, personality traits, education, attitude, spiritual)
ADL: activities of daily living; IADL: instrumental activities of daily living.
