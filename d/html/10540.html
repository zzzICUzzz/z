Index Terms
  • Bromhexine Hydrochloride
Pharmacologic Category
  • Mucolytic Agent
Reported Use

Acute and chronic bronchopulmonary diseases associated with abnormal mucous secretion and impaired mucous transport

Dosage Range

Note: Use of bromhexine in children <6 years may be contraindicated in some countries.

Oral:

Children 2 to <6 years: 2 mg 3 times daily or 4 mg twice daily (maximum: 8 mg/day).

Children 6 to <12 years: 4 to 8 mg 3 times daily (maximum: 24 mg/day).

Children ≥12 years, Adolescents, and Adults: 8 to 16 mg 3 times daily (maximum: 48 mg/day).

Inhalation: Solution for nebulization (2 mg/mL):

Children 2 to <6 years: 1.3 mg (10 drops) twice daily.

Children 6 to <12 years: 2 mg (1 mL) twice daily.

Children ≥12 years and Adolescents: 4 mg (2 mL) twice daily.

Adults: 8 mg (4 mL) twice daily.

Product Availability

Product available in various countries; not currently available in the US

Dosage Forms

Solution, Oral, as hydrochloride: 4 mg/5 mL, 8 mg/5 mL

Syrup, Oral, as hydrochloride: 4 mg/5 mL, 8 mg/5 mL

Tablet Disintegrating, Oral, as hydrochloride: 8 mg

Tablet, Oral, as hydrochloride: 8 mg
