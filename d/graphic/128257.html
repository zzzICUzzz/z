Brain MRI of PML in a patient with chronic lymphocytic leukemia
76-year-old patient with CLL and PML. (A) Brain MRI axial FLAIR images show hyperintense signal in left inferior parietal lobule (asterisk). Note relatively little mass effect on adjacent atrium of left lateral ventricle. Pre-contrast (B) and post-contrast (C) T1-weighted images show nonenhancing hypointensity in theinferior parietal lobule. Trace diffusion-weighted image (D) and ADC map (E) show a rim of hyperintensity along the anterior margin of thelesion (arrow) but no corresponding hypointensity on the ADC image, indicating absence of reduced diffusivity.
MRI: magnetic resonance imaging; PML: progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy; CLL: chronic lymphocytic leukemia; FLAIR: fluid-attenuated inversion recovery; ADC: apparent diffusion coefficient.
