Consequences of vasodilation in patients with cirrhosis
The progressive vasodilation seen in patients with cirrhosis leads to the activation of endogenous vasoconstrictors, sodium and water retention, and increasing renal vasoconstriction. For additional details, refer to UpToDate content on pathogenesis of ascites in patients with cirrhosis.
