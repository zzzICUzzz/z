NADPH oxidase activation
Glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase is required for the production of NADPH, an essential component of the NADPH oxidase system (1). The phagocyte NADPH oxidase system generates O- 2 by transferring e- from NADPH to O2 (2). Superoxide is metabolized to H2O2 by superoxide dismutase (3). Hydrogen peroxide can follow different metabolic pathways inside the cell. Myeloperoxidase can convert it into HOCl (4), which is involved in the oxygen-dependent killing of microorganisms in combination with other reactive oxygen speciesÂ (5). Alternatively, hydrogen peroxide can be degraded to H2O and O2, thereby avoiding deleterious effect on the cell (6). Hydrogen peroxide can also diffuse outside the cell and can augment other defective cells (7).
