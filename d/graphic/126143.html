Approach to management of posthysterectomy pelvic abscess
CT: computed tomography.
* For discussion of choice of antibiotics and/or surgical approach, refer to related text on posthysterectomy abscess.
Minimally invasive drainage can be performed by an interventional radiologist using a percutaneous approach or surgically, with a minimally invasive technique (eg, laparoscopy, colpotomy), if possible.
Consultation with an infectious disease specialist is appropriate for women who do not improve clinically after 48 to 72 hours of intravenous antibiotics therapy in the setting of negative imaging.
