Progression of human coronary atherosclerosis II
Histopathology of human coronary plaque progression, 2 of 2. The thrombus of a plaque erosion occurs in the absence of rupture and may overlie a substrate of pathologic intimal thickening (top left) or fibrous cap atheroma (top right). Eruptive calcified nodules represent a rare form of coronary thrombus. Acute rupture may progress to healing (healed plaque rupture) with resolution of the luminal occlusion.
Ca2+: calcification; NC: necrotic core; FC: fibrous cap; Th: thrombus.
