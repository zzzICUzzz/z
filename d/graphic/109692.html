Cranial imaging in an infant with severe congenital Zika syndrome
Imaging findings of an infant with severe CZS.
(A) Three-dimensional CT reconstruction showing classic phenotypic pattern of fetal skull collapse with overlapping cranial sutures and prominent occipital protrusion.
(B) T2-weighted MRI showing simplified gyral pattern with a lissencephalic appearance of the brain.
CZS: congenital Zika syndrome; CT: computed tomography; MRI: magnetic resonance imaging.
From: Pool KL, Adachi K, Karnezis S, et al. Association between neonatal neuroimaging and clinical outcomes in Zika-exposed infants from Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. JAMA Netw Open 2019; 2:e198124.
