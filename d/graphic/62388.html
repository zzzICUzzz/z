Methods to slow bone loss in postmenopausal women
Effects of three interventions on distal forearm bone density in postmenopausal women with low baseline forearm bone density. Bone loss was significantly slowed or prevented with either exercise plus estrogen or exercise plus calcium supplementation. The former regimen was both more effective and associated with a higher incidence of side effects, such as breast tenderness and vaginal bleeding.
