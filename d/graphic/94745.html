Huntington procedure for correction of puerperal uterine inversion
In the Huntington procedure, theÂ cup formed by the inversion is located. A clamp, such as an Allis or Babcock clamp, is placed on each round ligament entering the cup, approximately 2 cm deep in the cup. Gently pulling on the clamps exerts upward traction on the inverted fundus. Clamping and traction are repeated until the inversion is corrected. The myometrium can be clamped if the round ligaments cannot be identified.
