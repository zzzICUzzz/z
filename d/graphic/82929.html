Prevalence of atrial fibrillation by sex and age
Lifetime risk for developing atrial fibrillation (AF) from the Framingham Heart Study. Men and women without AF at 40 years of age were determined to have a 26 and 23 percent likelihood of developing incident AF by 80 years of age.
