Old tympanostomy tube with squamous debris
Extrusion of most tubes is initiated by the continual shedding of squamous debris from the epithelium of the tympanic membrane. As debris accumulates under the outer flange of the tube, the pressure causes the flange to lift up from the surface of the membrane, as depicted above.
