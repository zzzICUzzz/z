Variable periodicity of periodic limb movements of sleep in children
Recordings of leg activity in a 10-year-old male with periodic limb movement disorder (PLMD)and a strong family history of restless legs syndrome (RLS). Each polysomnographic segment is 3.5 minutes long.
Top panel: An example of high-periodicity leg movement activity, with consecutive intervals in the approximate range of 20 to 40 seconds.
Bottom panel: An example of low-periodicity leg movement activity in the same child.
L: left; R: right; EEG: electroencephalogram; EOG: electrooculogram; EMG: electromyogram; PTAF: pressure transducer airflow sensor; Therm: thermocouple (measures temperature); Chest: chest inductance plethysmography; Abdomen: abdomen inductance plethysmography.
