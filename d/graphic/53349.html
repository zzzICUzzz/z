Typical facies in Alagille syndrome
The typical facial features of Alagille syndrome are triangular, with a prominent forehead, deep-set eyes, a pointed chin, and a straight nose with a bulbous tip. The facial features change over time, with a more triangular shape during childhood and prominence of the jaw as patients reach adulthood.
