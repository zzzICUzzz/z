Evaluation of the child with suspected growing pains
Growing pains refer to recurrent, self-limited episodes of limb pain, usually affecting both lower extremities, in children of preschool and early school years. These pains occur late in the day and are frequently nocturnal and awaken the child from sleep. There is an absence of substantial musculoskeletal abnormalities, and these pains do not affect usual activities and function.

CBC: complete blood count; CRP: C-reactive protein; ESR: erythrocyte sedimentation rate.
