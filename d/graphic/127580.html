Stenting for an elastic venous lesion
The angiograms show the treatment of an elastic venous lesion with astent. The untreated lesion can be seen at the junction between the subclavian and brachiocephalic veins (A; arrow). The initial angioplasty shows complete effacement of the angioplasty balloon (B); however, the lesion shows marked elastic recoil (dashed arrow). Following placement of astent (arrowheads), the vessel is widely patent.
