Membranoproliferative glomerulonephritis
Light micrograph of membranoproliferative glomerulonephritis (MPGN) showing thickening of all capillary walls with double contours (long arrows) and focal areas of cellular proliferation (short arrow). The double-contour or tram-track appearance represents interposition of mesangial cell elements with new glomerular basement membrane synthesis.

Normal glomerulus
Light micrograph of a normal glomerulus. There are onlyone or twocells per capillary tuft, the capillary lumens are open, the thickness of the glomerular capillary wall (long arrow) is similar to that of the tubular basement membranes (short arrow), and the mesangial cells and mesangial matrix are located in the central or stalk regions of the tuft (arrows).
