Sickle SCAN point-of-care test for detection of hemoglobin S and hemoglobin C
(Panel A) Steps in testing. A blood sample (approximately 5 microL) is taken from a finger prick or a previously collected sample, added to a prefilled buffer solution, mixed,five drops (after discarding the firstthree drops) applied to the test strip cartridge (at the spot indicated "S"), and allowed to migrate by lateral flow. The result is scored afterfive minutes.
(Panel B) Test results for common hemoglobin patterns. A band indicates the presence of the hemoglobin listed. A band must be present at the final control (labeled "Ctrl") for the test to be valid.
SCD: sickle cell disease; POC: point-of-care; Ctrl: control;Hb: hemoglobin.
Modified from: McGann PT, Schaefer BA, Paniagua M, et al. Characteristics of a rapid, point-of-care lateral flow immunoassay for the diagnosis of sickle cell disease.
