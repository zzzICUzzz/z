Gallbladder anatomy
The gallbladder has four anatomic segments: fundus, body, neck, and infundibulum. Some authors use the terms neck and infundibulum synonymously, but some use the term infundibulum to describe the distal area at which the neck connects to the cystic duct. These two triangles are commonly referred to in cholecystectomy techniques.
