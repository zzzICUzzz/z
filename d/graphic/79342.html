Prevalence of HLA-DR4 in children with chronic arthritis
Among children with juvenile rheumatoid arthritis (JRA), the proportion of the study population with the HLA-DR4 allele is shown by disease-onset type (top graph) and byÂ sex (bottom). The horizontal line shows the frequency of the allele in the control group. HLA-DR4 was less common than in controls in younger males and patients with oligoarticular disease and more common than in controls in older children with polyarticular disease.
HLA: human leukocyte antigen.
Data from Murray KJ, Moroldo MB, Donnelly P, et al. Age-specific Effects of Juvenile Rheumatoid Arthritis-Associated HLA Alleles. Arthritis Rheum 1999; 42:1843.
