Importance of the pattern of GnRH secretion
Serum LH and FSH concentrations in an ovariectomized monkey. Pulsatile administration (given for six minutes every hour) of GnRH maintains serum FSH and LH concentrations. In comparison, a continuous infusion of GnRH (middle panel) leads to rapid and reversible suppression of both LH and FSH release.
FSH: follicle-stimulating hormone; GnRH: gonadotropin-releasing hormone; LH: luteinizing hormone.
