Left atrial abnormalities and complex aortic plaque correlate with the risk of thromboembolism in atrial fibrillation
Correlation of clinical risk for thromboembolism (TE) and transesophageal echocardiographic (TEE) findings in 786 patients with atrial fibrillation. Patients were deemed to be at high risk if they had one or more of the following clinical features: prior TE, women >75 years of age, systolic blood pressure >160 mmHg, and heart failure or poor left ventricular function. Patients with none of these features were either at low risk or, if they had a history of hypertension, moderate risk.
