12-lead electrocardiogram (ECG) showing preexcited atrial fibrillation (AF) due to Mahaim fibers
The transmission of atrial impulses to the ventricle is via an atriofascicular (Mahaim) fiber, resulting in a ventricular rate that averages 350 beats/min. The QRS complexes are wide or aberrant and have a left bundle branch block morphology and a left axis deviation, suggesting an insertion at the right ventricular apex.
