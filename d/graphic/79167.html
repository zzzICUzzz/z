Pustular psoriasis
This 28-year-old woman developed fever, hypotension, leukocytosis, widespread erythema, and pustules at 34 weeks gestation during her first pregnancy. She was hospitalized for several months, and the eruption resolved within the first month after delivery of a normal full-term infant. A skin biopsy showed changes typical of pustular psoriasis.
