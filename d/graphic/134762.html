Crash sign
Schematic diagrams showing (A) car stationed away from wall, representing mesencephalon (car) and occipital bone (wall) in normal fetuses, and (B) reversed into wall, representing posterior displacement of mesencephalon and deformation against occipital bone ("crash sign") in fetuses with open spina bifida. Ultrasound images in axial view at 12 to 13 weeks ofgestation, showing mesencephalon in normal fetuses (C,E,G) and crash sign in fetuses with open spina bifida (D,F,H). Three-dimensional reconstructed images of two sets of monochorionic twins discordant for spina bifida: Images of singleton fetuses without (G) and with (H) crash sign (arrow).
1, thalamus; 2, aqueduct; 3, mesencephalon; 4, arachnoid space; 5, occipital bone.
From: Ushakov F, Sacco A, Andreeva E, et al. Crash sign: new first-trimester sonographic marker of spina bifida.
