Primary ciliary dyskinesia in a child
Chest radiograph at the first presentation of a child known to have left atrial isomerism. He was first referred at four years of age withÂ a history of chronic wet cough for more than two years. He never had a prior chest radiograph. The chest radiograph shows collapse of the right lower lobe, which re-expanded after bronchoscopy and intravenous antibiotics. The bronchoscopy showed left pulmonary isomerism and endobronchial secretions. The ciliary biopsy showed immotile cilia, confirming the diagnosis of primary ciliary dyskinesia (PCD). The child also had heterotaxy syndrome, which is commonly associated with PCD.
