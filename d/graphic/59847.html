Splinting material
Common splinting materials include cotton padding (eg, WebrilÂ®), plaster, and stockinette (A). Layers of plaster are placed between layers of padding (B). Padding between the skin and plaster must be thick enough to prevent excessive pressure and injury but thin enough to allow adequate immobilization. Usually four layers is sufficient. A stockinette may be placed over the splint (C and D).
